4 Types of Loans

Business Loans

Business loans are used to finance the operations of a business. These loans can be secured or unsecured and are typically larger in size than personal loans.

Student Loans

Student loans are used to finance higher education. These loans can be either federal or private, and they typically have lower interest rates than other types of loans.

Auto Loans

Auto loans are used to finance the purchase of a vehicle. These loans are secured by the vehicle itself, which means that if the borrower defaults on the loan, the lender can repossess the car.

Mortgage Loans

Mortgage loans are used to finance the purchase of a home. These loans are secured by the property itself, which means that if the borrower defaults on the loan, the lender can foreclose on the property.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I do pre payment of a loan?

Yes, you can pre pay your loan. For this, there will be no pre payment charges on the floating rate of interest. However, charges will applied on fixed rate of interest.

How important in credit worthiness in loan approval?

It is advisable to not apply for too many loans, limit yourself according to the necessity. Credit score will go down when you apply for more loans.

What is an ongoing fee?

Ongoing fees are any regular payments charged by your lender in addition to the interest they apply including annual fees, monthly account keeping fees and offset fees.

What is a line of credit?

A line of credit is a type of loan that lets you borrow money up to a pre-set limit. You do not have to use the funds for a specific purpose.

Does a borrower need good credit?

No! A car title loan is not based upon a borrower's credit history. The loan is secured by the vehicle. However, if the borrower does not repay the loan, the lender can take possession of the vehicle.

What is a home equity loan?

A home equity loan lets you borrow a fixed amount, secured by the equity in your home, and receive your money in one lump sum. Normally, home equity loans have a fixed interest rate and a fixed term.
