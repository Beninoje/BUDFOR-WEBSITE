Frequently Asked Questions

How do I know if I need a budget?

Having a budget can be a very useful tool in developing a spending plan for your money. They allow you to get a sense of control over your finances, and enable you to get the most out of your cash. No matter your income, a budget can help you reach your financial goals faster.

What are the characteristics of a good budget?

At minimum, every good budget should:

  • Be accurate and inclusive of your spending habits
  • Have input from all affected parties (such as a spouse or partner)
  • Include attainable savings goals
  • Get reviewed and updated regularly

    • Why is an emergency fund so important?

    Emergency funds enable you to cover life's unexpected expenses, without going into a financial crisis. These may include major auto repairs, unplanned travel costs, and even sudden tax hikes. Don't risk losing financial stability, if you haven't done so already, begin contributing to an emergency fund.

    How do I calculate my net income?

    Your net income can be calculated using the following formula: (net income) = (gross income) - (deductions).

    Note. Gross income is your total income earned, and deductions include all your expenses.

    How much income should I spend on rent?

    Most experts would agree to follow the 30% Rule. It says you should spend no more than 30% of your gross income on rent. This figure should allow you some room to save, invest, and continue to grow financially.

    What are fixed and variable expenses?

    Fixed expenses are consistent such as monthly rent or mortgage costs. On the other hand, variable expenses are inconsistent such as grocery or gas costs.
