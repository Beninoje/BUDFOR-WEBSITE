Frequently Asked Questions

Who Needs To Pay Taxes?

Any individual or business that earns income is required to pay taxes. The specific types of taxes you'll need to pay depend on your income and business structure.

What Is A Tax Return?

A tax return is a form that you fill out and submit to the government to report your income and calculate how much tax you owe. If you overpaid your taxes during the year, you'll receive a refund, and if you underpaid, you'll need to pay the remaining balance.

What Is The Difference Between A Tax Credit And A Tax Deduction?

A tax credit is a dollar-for-dollar reduction in the amount of taxes you owe, while a tax deduction reduces your taxable income. Tax credits are generally more valuable than tax deductions because they directly reduce your tax liability.

How Can I Reduce My Tax Liability?

To reduce your tax liability, you can take advantage of deductions and credits, contribute to tax-advantaged retirement accounts, and adjust your withholding to ensure that you're not overpaying throughout the year. It's also important to keep accurate financial records and work with a qualified tax professional to help you navigate the tax code.

What Happens If I Don't Pay My Taxes?

If you don't pay your taxes, you may be subject to penalties and interest, and the government may take legal action to collect the owed amount. It's important to file your taxes on time and pay any owed taxes to avoid these consequences.

What Is A Tax Bracket?

A tax bracket is a range of income levels that are taxed at a specific rate. In the United States, there are seven federal tax brackets that range from 10% to 37%, depending on your income level. When you file your taxes, your income is taxed at the rate that corresponds to your tax bracket.
