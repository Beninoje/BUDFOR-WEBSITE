Frequently Asked Questions

Why should I invest my money?

Investing is just one way that you can get your money to work for you. It gives you the potential to build your wealth and outpace inflation. Though, it is always important to consider the risk-return tradeoff.

How can I invest without losing money to fees?

There are many avenues to investing that involve little to no commission fees. For instance, many big banks offer young trader accounts which typically offer free trading to individuals 25 and under. There are also many online-only brokerages which, thanks to lower operating costs, offer very low (or sometimes non-existent) trading commissions.

How do I manage investment risk?

The best way to manage investment risk is to tailor your portfolio to your comfort level. Sit down with an advisor and discuss your risk tolerance. They will be able to tell you what you should and shouldn't do with your hard-earned cash.

What is my investment time frame?

Your investment time or investment horizon is indicative from your goals and ultimately your age. Compound growth takes time and isn't always linear. Consider how much time you have to grow your money and design an investment plan that works for your situation â€” that is your time frame.

What exactly are GICs?

Guaranteed Investment Certificates (GICs) are very safe and highly secure investments that offer guaranteed returns on your principal. Although many different subsets of GICs exist, most offer you a return of interest on top of your principal investment amount at the end of a predetermined term.

GICs are simple and easy-to-understand investment vehicles, especially for beginners or investors with lower risk tolerances.

When should I meet with a financial advisor?

Unless you are an experienced and educated investor, you should meet with a financial advisor at least once per year. Advisors can help you understand risk, the current market, and different investment portfolios.
